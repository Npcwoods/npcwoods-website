Triage first

Text if it seems uncomplicated. Go in for red flags.

Good telehealth starts with knowing when not to use telehealth. These warning signs should be evaluated in person.

Text NPCWoods if you have:

  • Burning when you urinate.
  • Going more often than usual.
  • Strong urgency with small amounts.
  • Lower belly pressure without severe illness.
  • Prior UTI symptoms that feel familiar.

Go in person now for:

  • Fever or chills.
  • Back or side pain.
  • Pregnancy or possible pregnancy.
  • Severe vomiting or dehydration.
  • Significant blood in urine.
  • Symptoms in a young child.

Quick answers

Questions before you text.

Can you review UTI symptoms without a video call?

Yes, for many uncomplicated adult UTI symptoms, a structured text history can be appropriate. Chris will ask follow-up questions and will redirect you to in-person care if the answers point that way.

How much is the visit?

The NPCWoods text visit is $59. The price is shown clearly before you start, and Chris will tell you if your symptoms need in-person evaluation instead.

How fast will I hear back?

Most patients hear back the same day, usually within a few hours. If symptoms sound urgent, do not wait for a text visit. Use urgent care or the ER.

What happens if text-based care is not the right fit?

Chris will say so plainly and point you toward the level of care that fits your symptoms. The goal is the right care, not forcing every case into a text visit.
