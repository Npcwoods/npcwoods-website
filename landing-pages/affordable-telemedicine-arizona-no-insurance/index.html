Clear red flags

When not to text

Some symptoms need hands-on care.

Text-based telemedicine is useful, but it has limits. That honesty protects patients and the practice.

Seek urgent or emergency care for:

Chest pain, severe shortness of breath, fainting, stroke-like symptoms, severe one-sided weakness, severe abdominal pain, pregnancy-related urgent symptoms, uncontrolled bleeding, or symptoms that feel life-threatening.

FAQ

Arizona telemedicine questions

How much does NPCWoods cost in Arizona?

The visit is $59 flat. That covers Chris reviewing your symptoms, deciding whether telemedicine is appropriate, and sending a treatment plan if clinically appropriate.

Can Chris send prescriptions to Arizona pharmacies?

Yes. Chris is licensed in Arizona and can send appropriate non-controlled prescriptions electronically to local pharmacies after a clinical review.

What conditions can be handled by text?

Common fits include UTI symptoms, sinus infection symptoms, strep exposure or symptoms, pink eye, yeast infection, cold sores, mild skin infections, and some medication refills.

What if I need urgent in-person care?

If your symptoms sound like an emergency or need hands-on evaluation, Chris will tell you to seek in-person care instead of trying to force a text visit.
