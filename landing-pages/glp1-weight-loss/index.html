When Oral GLP-1 May Not Be Right

I'm a nurse practitioner, not a salesman. Not everyone is a candidate for GLP-1 medication, and I want you to know that upfront before you spend $59.

This medication is NOT appropriate if:

  • Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC) or MEN 2 syndrome
  • Active pancreatitis or history of severe pancreatitis
  • Currently pregnant, breastfeeding, or actively trying to conceive
  • Known allergy to semaglutide
  • BMI under 27 without weight-related health conditions
  • Severe gastroparesis or serious GI conditions

If none of those apply and your BMI is 30+ (or 27+ with a weight-related condition like high blood pressure or type 2 diabetes), you're likely a good candidate. Text us and we'll figure it out together.

Common Questions

Can I really get GLP-1 medication by text?

Yes. Chris Woods is a licensed nurse practitioner who evaluates you via text message. He'll review your health history, medications, and weight loss goals. If oral GLP-1 is appropriate, he sends the prescription to your local pharmacy. No video call, no clinic visit, no waiting room.

Is the oral pill as effective as the injection?

Clinical trials (OASIS 4) showed 16.6% average body weight loss with full adherence — comparable to injectable forms. 30% of patients lost 20% or more. The oral version delivers the same class of medication in a daily tablet instead of a weekly shot.

What are the side effects?

Most common is nausea, especially early on. Some patients experience mild GI symptoms like diarrhea or constipation. These typically decrease as your body adjusts. Chris starts you on a low dose and increases gradually to minimize side effects. Serious side effects are rare and will be discussed during your consultation.

How much does the whole thing cost?

$59 for the consultation (every 2 months). Your first month includes a prescription called into your local pharmacy. Month 2, Chris checks in with you for free and calls in a refill — no charge. After that, it's $59 again for your next consultation. Medication starts at $149/month and up depending on dose. No memberships, no platform fees, no hidden charges. Cancel anytime.

How is this different from other telehealth weight loss companies?

Most competitors charge $200–400/month in membership fees before you even get the medication. They use chatbots to screen you and you never talk to the same provider twice. At NPCWoods, there's no membership — just a $59 consultation fee and medication starting at $149/month. Chris personally evaluates every patient. No contracts, cancel anytime.

How fast will I hear back?

Most people hear back within a few hours. Chris personally reads every text — no call centers, no chatbots. Same-day evaluations are common.

How do I take the medication?

One pill first thing in the morning on an empty stomach with a small sip of water. Wait 30 minutes before eating or drinking anything else. That's the whole routine — easier than making coffee.
