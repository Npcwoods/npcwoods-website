When to Go to the ER Instead

I'm a Nurse Practitioner, not a salesman. Strep is usually straightforward, but a few patterns mean you need to be seen in person right away, not texting me.

Head to the ER if any of these apply:

  • Trouble breathing, drooling because you can't swallow, or a muffled "hot potato" voice, can be a sign of an abscess or airway swelling
  • Severe one-sided throat swelling, or you can't open your mouth fully (trismus)
  • Stiff neck along with a high fever, rule out meningitis
  • A sandpaper-feeling rash with high fever, could be scarlet fever needing prompt evaluation
  • Severe dehydration because you can't keep fluids down
  • A child under 3 with high fever and significant throat pain

For the classic strep picture, sudden severe sore throat, fever, swollen tender lymph nodes, white patches on the tonsils, no cough, that's bread-and-butter telehealth, and we handle it routinely.

Common Questions

Can strep throat be treated without a swab?

Yes, a licensed Nurse Practitioner can diagnose and treat strep throat online without a throat swab by utilizing the clinically validated modified Centor criteria scoring system. We evaluate signs like high fever, white patches on tonsils, and swollen lymph nodes to determine if immediate antibiotic therapy is appropriate.

What if it's not strep, just a viral sore throat?

Most sore throats are viral and do not require antibiotics, which we will explain clearly if your symptoms suggest a cold. Chris provides a supportive symptom care plan. If we rule out strep throat, we will detail how to manage your pain safely with over-the-counter options.

What antibiotic will I get?

First-line antibiotics for strep throat are penicillin or amoxicillin, which have excellent success rates and are highly affordable. Chris selects alternative options for penicillin allergies. These common generic prescriptions typically cost under fifteen dollars at local pharmacies with a GoodRx discount card.

How much does the visit and the antibiotic cost?

Every online urgent care visit at NPCWoods costs a fifty-nine dollar flat fee. There are no monthly subscription fees, surprise co-pays, or hidden paperwork charges. This affordable cash rate covers your entire clinical evaluation and all follow-up communication regarding this treatment cycle.

How fast will I hear back?

Most patients hear back and receive a treatment plan within a few hours of submission. Chris Woods reviews all secure text consults same-day, including weekends. Once the double board-certified Nurse Practitioner completes the clinical review, prescriptions are sent electronically to your pharmacy immediately.
