North Carolina Telehealth Disclosure & Limitations

Asynchronous (text-based) telemedicine visits at NPCWoods have unique clinical limitations compared to traditional in-person care. Visits are conducted via secure, interactive text messaging and do not include real-time video feeds or a hands-on physical exam. If your symptoms are clinically complex, require a physical diagnostic test, or fall outside the safe parameters of asynchronous care, you will be directed to an in-person healthcare provider, and your visit fee will be fully refunded. Prescriptions are sent to your local NC pharmacy only when clinically appropriate; controlled substances are not prescribed.

How It Works

Three steps. No waiting rooms.

1

Text us your symptoms

Tap the button to send a text message. Tell us what's going on: UTI, sinus pressure, ear pain, whatever it is. We'll ask a few follow-up questions to understand your situation.

2

Chris reviews your case

Chris Woods, a double board-certified Family Nurse Practitioner licensed in North Carolina, personally reviews your symptoms and creates a treatment plan. No video call required. Everything happens by text.

3

Prescription sent to your pharmacy

If you need medication, your prescription is sent electronically to any pharmacy in North Carolina: CVS, Walgreens, Walmart, Ingles (Western NC), Harris Teeter, wherever is closest. Usually ready within 1–2 hours.

Can a North Carolina Nurse Practitioner prescribe antibiotics online? +

Yes, a licensed Family Nurse Practitioner in North Carolina can prescribe antibiotics online. Chris Woods at NPCWoods reviews symptoms via secure text consults and sends appropriate prescriptions same-day to your preferred local pharmacy.

Is text-based telemedicine legal in North Carolina? +

Yes, text-based asynchronous telemedicine is fully legal and regulated in North Carolina. NPCWoods operates in complete compliance with state medical board guidelines, providing secure, HIPAA-compliant consults without requiring video appointments.

How long does an online urgent care visit take in North Carolina? +

A text-based urgent care consult in North Carolina takes under five minutes to start. Once submitted, our double board-certified Nurse Practitioner reviews your symptoms and sends your prescription within hours.
