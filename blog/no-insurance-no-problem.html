Why People Avoid Going In

Let's call it what it is: the healthcare system wasn't built for people paying out of pocket. When you're paying out of pocket:

  • You fear surprise bills. We've all heard the horror stories: $500 for a 10-minute visit, $200 lab fees that show up weeks later. So you avoid going altogether.
  • You don't know what's available. Most people think their only options are the ER or a regular clinic, both of which are expensive without a plan.
  • You wait too long. That sinus infection turns into something worse. That anxiety builds. That UTI becomes a kidney infection. Waiting doesn't save you money. It costs you more in the long run.

Here's the part nobody talks about: roughly 27 million Americans avoid going in because of cost and hassle. You're not alone, and you're definitely not out of options.
