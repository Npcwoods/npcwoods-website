What I Can Treat (And What I Can't)

Strep throat is straightforward when you catch it early. Here's what I can handle, and what you need to escalate:

💊

Classic Strep

Fever, sore throat, white patches on tonsils, pain on swallowing. First or second occurrence. This is the bread and butter — amoxicillin or penicillin, done.

🔄

Recurrent Strep

Your kid gets strep multiple times a year. I can treat each episode, but if it's more than 4-5 times yearly, a formal tonsil evaluation might be worth it.

⚠️

Strep + Complications

Severe swelling (trouble breathing), signs of abscess, very high fever, or unexplained rash? You need in-person evaluation. I'll recommend urgent care or ED.

Important Limits

I can't confirm strep with a rapid test — you'll need to either do a home rapid test or get one at urgent care. I evaluate based on symptoms and history. If your child has severe airway compromise, worsening despite antibiotics, or signs of rheumatic fever, this isn't the right place for treatment.

These Might Be Complicated. Worth an Evaluation.

  • Fever over 104°F
  • Difficulty swallowing liquids or saliva (drooling)
  • Neck stiffness or pain
  • Enlarged tonsils blocking the airway (talk differently?)
  • Recent strep antibiotic (and symptoms still severe)
  • Rash along with sore throat (scarlet fever pattern)
  • Stomach pain or vomiting

Next step: Text me the details. I may recommend urgent care for in-person exam or rapid test.

These Are Red Flags. Go to the ER or Urgent Care Now.

  • Trouble breathing or stridor (high-pitched breathing sound)
  • Difficulty swallowing even saliva (inability to manage secretions)
  • Severe swelling of throat, jaw, or neck
  • Drooling (can't manage saliva)
  • Unconscious, unresponsive, or disoriented
  • Suspected epiglottitis or retropharyngeal abscess
  • Fever over 105°F with confusion

Don't wait for text-based care. Call 911 or go to the ER immediately.

What if my kid needs a rapid strep test? ⋮

I can't do a rapid test remotely, but you have options: CVS, Walgreens, and most urgent cares do them ($10–30). You can also get a rapid strep test kit mailed to you. If you're pretty sure it's strep based on symptoms, we can start treatment and confirm with a test later. The clinical picture is usually clear enough in kids.

What antibiotic will my kid get? ⋮

Usually amoxicillin or penicillin V. These are the first-line antibiotics for strep and work great. If your child is allergic to penicillin, I have other options: azithromycin (Z-pack) or cephalexin. I'll ask about allergies upfront and tailor the prescription to your kid.

How long does it take to feel better? ⋮

Most kids feel significantly better within 24–48 hours of starting antibiotics. Fever usually breaks within 24 hours. Sore throat and swallowing pain improve over a few days. By day 3, most kids are back to normal — eating, playing, ready to return to school (they're non-contagious after 24 hours of antibiotics).

Can you follow up if my kid isn't getting better? ⋮

Absolutely. Text me anytime. If antibiotics don't help after 3–4 days, or if symptoms get worse, I'll reevaluate. Sometimes it's not strep — could be a virus, or a different bacterial infection. I don't charge for follow-up texts or adjustments. We figure it out together.

What about my kid's regular pediatrician? ⋮

I'm not replacing them. Think of me as a bridge: when you're stuck and can't get an appointment, I help now. You should still have a regular pediatrician for preventive care, long-term stuff, and big-picture health.
